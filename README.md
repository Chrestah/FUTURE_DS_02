# FUTURE_DS_02
## Overview

This project presents a customer retention and churn analysis dashboard designed to help understand customer behavior
identify churn patterns, and highlight key retention drivers.The analysis translates raw customer data into actionable
business insights to support strategic decision-making.

 ### Objectives
-Identify key drivers of customer churn

-Analyze retention patterns across customer segments

-Evaluate customer lifetime behavior trends

-Provide actionable recommendations to improve retention

-Support data-driven business decisions

### Key parttens 

1. Churn Patterns
   
   -Month-to-month contracts are the strongest predictor of churn
   
   -Electronic check payment users show significantly higher churn rates
   
   -Customers with higher monthly charges are more likely to churn
   
   -Fiber optic users demonstrate unexpectedly high churn levels
   
3. Retention Drivers
   
  Long-term contracts significantly improve customer retention
  
  Customers using automatic payment methods show lower churn
  
  Lower monthly charges are associated with improved retention
  
  Stable service usage patterns correlate with loyalty
  
3. Customer Lifetime Trend
   
  -New customers (low tenure) are most vulnerable to churn
  
  -Churn rate decreases steadily as customer tenure increases
  
  -Long-term customers contribute disproportionately to stable revenue
   
4. Business Recommendations
   
 - Businesss should encourage migration from month-to-month to long-term contracts,
   
   Promote automatic payment methods to reduce friction-based churn,
   
   Review pricing strategy for high-churn customer segments,
   
   Investigate service quality issues in fiber optic customer segment,
   
   Strengthen onboarding experience to improve early-stage retention

### Dashboard Scope

The dashboard explores churn behavior across:

-Contract types

-Payment methods

-Monthly charges

-Internet service types

-Customer tenure
